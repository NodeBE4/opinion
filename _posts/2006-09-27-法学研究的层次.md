   <h1>
    法学研究的层次
   </h1>
   <ul class="post-entry-meta">
    <li>
     By:
     <a href="http://www.ideobook.com/about-ideobook/" title="查看 爱德布克 的作者主页">
      爱德布克
     </a>
     . 2006-9-27.
     <a href="http://www.ideobook.com/372/post-views-count/" title="统计说明">
      7,192
     </a>
    </li>
   </ul>
  </header>
  <div class="post-entry-content">
   <p>
    九月廿一日，佛罗里达州立大学法学院的 J. B. Ruhl 教授在其所属的群组博克
    <a href="http://jurisdynamics.blogspot.com">
     Jurisdynamics
    </a>
    上发表了一则文字：“
    <a href="http://jurisdynamics.blogspot.com/2006/09/hierarchy-of-legal-scholarship.html">
     The Hierarchy of Legal Scholarship
    </a>
    ”，将法学研究依由低到高的层次分为如下10+1级：
   </p>
   <blockquote>
    <p>
     0 – Blog posts
     <br/>
     1 – Publication of what are essentially blog posts with footnotes
     <br/>
     2 – Doctrinal review of the state of the law
     <br/>
     3 – Doctrinal study of interesting questions of law
     <br/>
     4 – Doctrinal synthesis of developments in law
     <br/>
     5 – Normative policy analysis of law
     <br/>
     6 – Normative policy analysis of law with substantial reform proposals
     <br/>
     7 – Legal theory
     <br/>
     8 – “Law and” interdisciplinary studies
     <br/>
     9 – Empirical study of legal institutions
     <br/>
     10 – Empirical study of law’s impact on society
    </p>
   </blockquote>
   <p>
    J. B. Ruhl 教授的这一“blog post”引起了法学教授博克圈的不少讨论，例如
    <a href="http://lsolum.typepad.com/legaltheory/2006/09/chen_on_the_hie.html">
     Lawrence Solum
    </a>
    、
    <a href="http://prawfsblawg.blogs.com/prawfsblawg/2006/09/jim_chens_heira.html">
     Michael Dimino
    </a>
    、
    <a href="http://jurisdynamics.blogspot.com/2006/09/value-based-hierarchy-of-legal.html">
     Jim
    </a>
    <a href="http://jurisdynamics.blogspot.com/2006/09/scholarly-hierarchies-further-thoughts.html">
     Chen
    </a>
    、
    <a href="http://www.discourse.net/archives/2006/09/hierarchies_of_legal_articles_and_the_reproduction_thereof.html">
     Michael Froomkin
    </a>
    ，还有来自 Eric Muller 教授的一个
    <a href="http://www.isthatlegal.org/archives/2006/09/eric_mullers_hi.html">
     幽默/戏仿版
    </a>
    ，相当有趣：
   </p>
   <blockquote>
    <p>
     0 – Student-written work by people other than me.
     <br/>
     1 – Articles using two or more of the following four words or phrases: “intersectionality,” “rent-seeking,” “hegemony,” “externality.”
     <br/>
     2 – Work in my field that totally ought to cite my work but fails to do so.
     <br/>
     3 – First novels by professors at Yale Law School.
     <br/>
     4 – Student-written work by me.
     <br/>
     5 – Work that cites my work.
     <br/>
     6 – My work.
     <br/>
     7 – Scholarship ranking scholars, schools, and scholarship.
     <br/>
     8 – Scholarship by faculty on the appointments committees of schools from which I’m hoping for lateral offers.
     <br/>
     9 – Revolver, The Beatles. (UK version.)
     <br/>
     10 – Articles ranking scholarship ranking scholars, schools, and scholarship
     <br/>
     [11 – My blog post.]
    </p>
   </blockquote>
   <p>
    与此相关的还有数日前 Brian Leiter 在 Yale Law Journal 网站发表的一则短文：“
    <a href="http://thepocketpart.org/09/20/06/leiter.html">
     Why Blogs Are Bad for Legal Scholarship
    </a>
    ”。
   </p>
