  <div class="post-entry-content">
   <p>
    圣约翰大学法学院
    <a href="http://new.stjohns.edu/academics/graduate/law/faculty/profiles/Tamanaha">
     Brian Z. Tamanaha
    </a>
    教授在 Balkinization 写了一篇“
    <a href="http://balkin.blogspot.com/2008/01/why-interdisciplinary-movement-in-legal.html">
     Why the Interdisciplinary Movement in Legal Academia Might be a Bad Idea (For Most Law Schools)
    </a>
    ”，引起一些美国同行的热议：
   </p>
   <p>
    1.
    <a href="http://leiterlawschool.typepad.com/leiter/2008/01/why-non-elite-1.html">
     Brian Leiter 收集的一些评论
    </a>
    ;
   </p>
   <p>
    2. “
    <a href="http://www.concurringopinions.com/archives/2008/01/is_interdiscipl.html">
     Is Interdisciplinary Legal Study a Luxury?
    </a>
    ” by Dan Solove;
   </p>
   <p>
    3. “
    <a href="http://prawfsblawg.blogs.com/prawfsblawg/2008/01/non-elite-inter.html">
     Non-Elite Interdisiciplinary Scholars
    </a>
    ” by Ethan Leib;
   </p>
   <p>
    4. “
    <a href="http://money-law.blogspot.com/2008/01/tamanaha-on-interdisciplinary.html">
     Tamanaha on Interdisciplinary Scholarship
    </a>
    ” by Belle Lettre;
   </p>
   <p>
    5. “
    <a href="http://lsolum.typepad.com/legaltheory/2008/01/interdisciplina.html">
     Interdisciplinarity, Multidisciplinarity, and the Future of the Legal Academy
    </a>
    ” by Lawrence B. Solum;
   </p>
   <p>
    6. “
    <a href="http://busmovie.typepad.com/ideoblog/2008/01/law-schools-sch.html">
     Law schools, scholarship, and lawyer licensing
    </a>
    ” by Larry Ribstein;
   </p>
   <p>
    7. “
    <a href="http://balkin.blogspot.com/2008/01/is-there-impending-crisis-in-non-elite.html">
     Is There an Impending Crisis in Non-Elite Law Schools?
    </a>
    ” by Brian Tamanaha;
   </p>
   <p>
    8. “
    <a href="http://www.concurringopinions.com/archives/2008/01/encore_i_couldn.html">
     Encore – I Couldn’t Resist Saying Something About the Interdisciplinarity Debate
    </a>
    ” by Jeffrey Lipshaw;
   </p>
   <p>
    9. “
    <a href="http://balkin.blogspot.com/2008/01/aesops-bat-and-interdisciplinary-legal.html">
     Aesop’s Bat and Interdisciplinary Legal Scholarship
    </a>
    ” by Mark Graber;
   </p>
   <p>
    10. “
    <a href="http://money-law.blogspot.com/2008/01/interdisciplinary-legal-education-overt.html">
     Interdisciplinary legal education: the overt costs
    </a>
    ” by Jim Chen;
   </p>
   <p>
    11. “
    <a href="http://outofthejungle.blogspot.com/2008/01/on-impact-of-blogging-and-legal.html">
     On the Impact of Blogging and Legal Scholarship
    </a>
    ” by Jim Milles.
