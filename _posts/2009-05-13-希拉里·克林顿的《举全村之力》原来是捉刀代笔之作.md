   <h1>
    希拉里·克林顿的《举全村之力》原来是捉刀代笔之作
   </h1>
   <ul class="post-entry-meta">
    <li>
     By:
     <a href="http://www.ideobook.com/ming-shen/" title="查看 沈明 的作者主页">
      沈明
     </a>
     . 2009-5-13.
     <a href="http://www.ideobook.com/372/post-views-count/" title="统计说明">
      5,219
     </a>
    </li>
   </ul>
  </header>
  <div class="post-entry-content">
   <p>
    <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B001SARD92?ie=UTF8&amp;tag=oceintelleweb-20&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=390957&amp;creativeASIN=B001SARD92">
     <img alt="It Takes a Village" src="/img/It_Takes_Village.jpg"/>
    </a>
    <a href="http://www.amazon.cn/mn/detailApp/ref=as_li_ss_tl?_encoding=UTF8&amp;tag=ideobook&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;asin=B00243GXFM&amp;camp=536&amp;creative=3132&amp;creativeASIN=B00243GXFM">
     <img alt="举全村之力" src="/img/It_Takes_Village_CN.jpg"/>
    </a>
   </p>
   <p>
    <a href="http://posner.ideobook.com">
     理查德·波斯纳
    </a>
    法官在
    <a href="/669/posner-little-book-of-plagiarism/">
     《论剽窃》
    </a>
    一书中写道：
   </p>
   <blockquote>
    <p>
     ……
    </p>
    <p>
     相比于教科书的情况，名义作者是政客、明星但实际上是捉刀人代笔的书和司法意见书之间有更多相似之处。（明星博客是最晚近的代笔明星写作的例子。）这里没有受害者。捉刀代笔人获得了报酬，而且由于不存在对于原创性的期待，公众也谈不上受愚弄。然而，对于书的捉刀代笔人的认同已经日渐普遍了，在这种情况下，如果不提及捉刀代笔人，就会给公众造成一种明星自己写作的印象，比如说希拉里·克林顿的书
     <a href="http://www.amazon.cn/mn/detailApp/ref=as_li_ss_tl?_encoding=UTF8&amp;tag=ideobook&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;asin=B00243GXFM&amp;camp=536&amp;creative=3132&amp;creativeASIN=B00243GXFM">
      《举全村之力》
     </a>
     （
     <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B001SARD92?ie=UTF8&amp;tag=oceintelleweb-20&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=390957&amp;creativeASIN=B001SARD92">
      <em>
       It Takes a Village
      </em>
     </a>
     ），作者与此书捉刀代笔人签订的合同就禁止公开她的身份。……
    </p>
   </blockquote>
   <p>
    5月14日更新：恰如所料，某些希粉（Hillary’s fans）不高兴了。还好，还没造成“中国不高兴”的严重后果。广告取消。附上维基百科 “
    <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/It_Takes_a_Village">
     It Takes a Village
    </a>
    ” 的相关段落（省略了注释）：
   </p>
   <blockquote>
    <p>
     <strong>
      Ghostwriter controversy
     </strong>
    </p>
    <p>
     Clinton has been criticized for not giving credit to a ghostwriter in connection with
     <em>
      It Takes a Village
     </em>
     . The majority of the book was reportedly written by ghostwriter Barbara Feinman.[8] When the book was first announced in April 1995,
     <em>
      The New York Times
     </em>
     reported publisher Simon &amp; Schuster as saying “The book will actually be written by Barbara Feinman, a journalism professor at Georgetown University in Washington. Ms. Feinman will conduct a series of interviews with Mrs. Clinton, who will help edit the resulting text.”[9]
     <p>
      Feinman spent seven months on the project and was paid $120,000 for her work.[10] Feinman, however, was not mentioned anywhere in the book. Clinton’s acknowledgment section began: “It takes a village to bring a book into the world, as everyone who has written one knows. Many people have helped me to complete this one, sometimes without even knowing it. They are so numerous that I will not even attempt to acknowledge them individually, for fear that I might leave one out.”[11] During her promotional tour for the book, Clinton said, “I actually wrote the book … I had to write my own book because I want to stand by every word.”[2] Clinton stated that Feinman assisted in interviews and did some editorial drafting of “connecting paragraphs”, while Clinton herself wrote the final manuscript in longhand.[2]
      <p>
       This led Feinman to complain at the time to
       <em>
        Capitol Style
       </em>
       magazine over the lack of acknowledgement.[12] In 2001,
       <em>
        The Wall Street Journal
       </em>
       reported that “New York literary circles are buzzing with vitriol over Sen. Clinton’s refusal, so far, to share credit with any writer who helps on her book.”[13] Later, in a 2002 article for
       <em>
        The Writer’s Chronicle
       </em>
       ,[14] Barbara Feinman Todd (now using her married name) related that the project with Clinton had gone smoothly, producing drafts in a round-robin style. Feinman agrees that Clinton was involved with the project, but also states that, “Like any first lady, Mrs. Clinton had an extremely hectic schedule and writing a book without assistance would have been logistically impossible.” Feinman reiterates that her only objection to the whole process was the lack of any acknowledgement. A 2005 Georgetown University web page bio for Barbara Feinman Todd states that It Takes a Village was one of “several high-profile books” that she has “assisted, as editor, writer and researcher.”[15]
      </p>
     </p>
    </p>
   </blockquote>
   <p>
    至少提供了一些事实报道。只要看看12万美元的价格就明白了，别的其实无须多说。
    <br/>
    <span id="more-856">
    </span>
    <br/>
    附带说明，本人对此事并无任何争论的兴趣。我只是怀疑，一些国人似乎并不完全清楚什么叫做“剽窃”（plagiarism）。对此感到迷惘的读者不妨看看波斯纳的
    <a href="/669/posner-little-book-of-plagiarism/">
     《论剽窃》
    </a>
    （
    <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/037542475X?ie=UTF8&amp;tag=oceintelleweb-20&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=390957&amp;creativeASIN=037542475X">
     <em>
      The Little Book of Plagiarism
     </em>
    </a>
    ），一本很有意思的小书，（又是广告，哈哈！）精于此道的专家学者和“希粉”们请绕行。谢谢。
   </p>
