 <div class="post-body">
  <p>
   第3届牛津当代中国研究研究生会议将于2014年6月30日至7月1日举办，现诚挚向海内外同学征稿。在学科研究和区域研究之间界限日益明晰的背景下，本次会议着重讨论当代中国研究的学科定位问题。与会者可享受免费食宿和英国内部交通补助。详情请见征稿通知（
   <a href="http://cnpolitics.org/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/OxCN_2014_CfP_06.pdf" target="_blank">
    点此阅读PDF版
   </a>
   ），欢迎扩散。
  </p>
  <p>
   <strong>
    Conference CfP: Beyond Boundaries: China in Disciplinary and Area Studies
   </strong>
  </p>
  <p>
   30 June – 1 July, 2014, University of Oxford
  </p>
  <p>
   The 3rd Oxford Graduate Conference on Contemporary China invites graduate students of all social science disciplines to share their views on the actual and ideal ‘academic positioning’ of Contemporary Chinese Studies through their own research.
  </p>
  <p>
   In addition to the presentations on their specific research questions, we encourage the participants to present how, and why, they choose to use China as a case to illustrate mainstream social scientific theories, as a counterexample to challenge the theories, or simply as an exception that has little to do with the theories.
  </p>
  <p>
   We encourage participants to demonstrate and justify their views on the relationship between Contemporary Chinese Studies, disciplinary studies, and area studies through their own methodological and theoretical contributions as well as empirical findings.
  </p>
  <p>
   In addition to the main conference on 1 July, there will also be a roundtable discussion with prominent China scholars including
   <strong>
    Professor Rosemary Foot, FBA
   </strong>
   ,
   <strong>
    Professor Vivienne Shue, FBA
   </strong>
   , and
   <strong>
    Professor Jeffrey Wasserstrom, Editor of the
    <em>
     Journal of Asian Studies
    </em>
   </strong>
   on the late afternoon on 30 June.
  </p>
  <p>
   Free accommodation will be provided for participants who come from outside Oxford. Free lunch, drinks, and refreshments will be available to all presenters. It is also possible to reimburse moderate domestic travelling cost. For more information, please see the
   <a href="http://cnpolitics.org/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/OxCN_2014_CfP_06.pdf" target="_blank">
    Call for Papers
   </a>
   .
  </p>
