 <div class="entry-content">
  <p>
   Stratechery launched on March 25, 2013 with the post
   <a href="http://stratechery.com/2013/welcome-to-stratechery/">
    Welcome to Stratechery
   </a>
   . While I had somewhat prepopulated the blog with old content (including
   <a href="http://stratechery.com/2010/the-ipad-its-for-everyone-else/">
    a piece on the original iPad
   </a>
   and a research paper on
   <a href="http://stratechery.com/2010/apple-innovators-dilemma/">
    Apple and the Innovator’s Dilemma
   </a>
   ), the vast majority of my 203 posts and linked-list items came after that date. Here’s a quick summary of the highlights:
  </p>
  <figure aria-describedby="caption-attachment-134" class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_134" style="width: 600px">
   <a href="http://stratechery.com/2013/welcome-to-stratechery/">
    <img alt='From "Welcome to Stratechy"' class="size-medium wp-image-134" height="450" src="https://stratechery.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/03/WelcomeToStratechery-Sailboats-600x450.jpg" width="600"/>
   </a>
   <figcaption class="wp-caption-text" id="caption-attachment-134">
    From “Welcome to Stratechy”
   </figcaption>
  </figure>
  <h4>
   The Five Most-Viewed Articles:
  </h4>
  <ol>
   <li>
    <a href="http://stratechery.com/2013/if-steve-ballmer-ran-apple/">
     If Steve Ballmer Ran Apple
    </a>
    – Most view Steve Ballmer’s tenure at Microsoft as a mixed bag: increased revenue and profits, but multiple missed opportunities. I argue these results are intertwined.
   </li>
   <li>
    <a href="http://stratechery.com/2013/the-deal-that-makes-no-sense/">
     The Deal That Makes No Sense
    </a>
    – Because this blog is a hobby, I rarely have the time to jump on a story as it breaks. This, written about Microsoft’s acquisition of Nokia, was an exception.
   </li>
   <li>
    <a href="http://stratechery.com/2013/jony-ive-is-not-a-graphic-designer/">
     Jony Ive is Not a Graphic Designer
    </a>
    – A short piece with an extended Jony quote and my predictions for the as-yet-unannounced iOS 7.
   </li>
   <li>
    <a href="http://stratechery.com/2013/why-microsofts-reorganization-is-a-bad-idea/">
     Why Microsoft’s Reorganization Is a Bad Idea
    </a>
    – Microsoft reorganized itself from a product-based company to a functionally-organized one. While the move makes sense in theory, I don’t think it fits at all with the reality of today’s Microsoft.
   </li>
   <li>
    <a href="http://stratechery.com/2013/whither-liberal-arts/">
     Whither Liberal Arts
    </a>
    – I was tremendously disappointed in the story Apple told – or, more accurately, failed to tell – about the iPad in its fall event. (The conclusions here are in marked contrast to the 6th most popular post,
    <a href="http://stratechery.com/2013/tim-cook-is-a-great-ceo/">
     Tim Cook is a Great CEO
    </a>
    ).
   </li>
  </ol>
  <figure aria-describedby="caption-attachment-430" class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_430" style="width: 600px">
   <a href="http://stratechery.com/2013/why-microsofts-reorganization-is-a-bad-idea/">
    <img alt="From &quot;Why Microsoft's Reorganization is a Bad Idea&quot;" class="size-medium wp-image-430" height="345" src="https://stratechery.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/07/functional-600x345.jpg" width="600"/>
   </a>
   <figcaption class="wp-caption-text" id="caption-attachment-430">
    From “Why Microsoft’s Reorganization is a Bad Idea”
   </figcaption>
  </figure>
  <h4>
   Five Big Ideas
  </h4>
  <ul>
   <li>
    <a href="http://stratechery.com/2013/clayton-christensen-got-wrong/">
     What Clayton Christensen Got Wrong
    </a>
    – While I’m a believer in the idea of new market disruption, I believe that modular disruption is
    <em>
     not
    </em>
    inevitable, particularly in consumer markets.
   </li>
   <li>
    <a href="http://stratechery.com/2013/the-uncanny-valley-of-a-functional-organization/">
     The Uncanny Value of a Functional Organization
    </a>
    – This was the second article I wrote about Microsoft’s reorganization, and while it got fewer page views, I think this one is better. It’s also where I first introduced the “Uncanny Valley” framework.
   </li>
   <li>
    <a href="http://stratechery.com/2013/the-ipad-and-the-disaggregation-of-computing/">
     The iPad and the Disaggregation of Computing
    </a>
    – The iPad isn’t replacing the general-purpose PC. Rather, it’s increasing the specialization of computing devices. Also see
    <a href="http://stratechery.com/2013/the-humpty-dumpty-pc/">
     The Humpty-Dumpty PC
    </a>
    and
    <a href="http://stratechery.com/2013/the-alleged-13-inch-ipad-and-the-triumph-of-thin-clients-finally/">
     The (Alleged) 13-Inch iPad and the Triumph of Thin Clients
    </a>
    .
   </li>
   <li>
    <a href="http://stratechery.com/2013/the-android-detour/">
     The Android Detour
    </a>
    – Android is a means to an end for Google, not a central focus of their business. It’s interesting how much less controversial this idea has become in the seven months since I wrote this. Also see
    <a href="http://stratechery.com/2013/understanding-google/">
     Understanding Google
    </a>
    .
   </li>
   <li>
    <a href="http://stratechery.com/2013/multitudes-social/">
     The Multitudes of Social
    </a>
    and
    <a href="http://stratechery.com/2013/socialcommunication-map/">
     The Social/Communications Map
    </a>
    – On the impact of mobile on social, and the reality that no one service can ever monopolize relationships.
   </li>
  </ul>
  <figure aria-describedby="caption-attachment-600" class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_600" style="width: 600px">
   <a href="http://stratechery.com/2013/socialcommunication-map/">
    <img alt='From "The Social/Communications Map"' class="size-medium wp-image-600" height="466" src="https://stratechery.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/11/photo-2-600x466.jpg" width="600"/>
   </a>
   <figcaption class="wp-caption-text" id="caption-attachment-600">
    From “The Social/Communications Map”
   </figcaption>
  </figure>
  <h4>
   Five More Favorites
  </h4>
  <ul>
   <li>
    <a href="http://stratechery.com/2013/apple-the-black-swan/">
     Apple the Black Swan
    </a>
    – Wall Street doesn’t understand Apple, because it bases its business on qualities that can’t be measured.
   </li>
   <li>
    <a href="http://stratechery.com/2013/the-intel-opportunity/">
     The Intel Opportunity
    </a>
    – Intel has a new CEO, who is facing a similar situation to that faced by Andy Grove when he becamse CEO in the 1980s.
   </li>
   <li>
    <a href="http://stratechery.com/2013/friction/">
     Friction
    </a>
    – The Internet is removing friction from everything it touches, but that’s not always a good thing.
   </li>
   <li>
    <a href="http://stratechery.com/2013/magical-ipad/">
     The Magical iPad
    </a>
    – The iPad is a truly unique device, able to transform into whatever you wish it to be.
   </li>
   <li>
    <a href="http://stratechery.com/2013/christmas-gifts-meaning-design/">
     Christmas Gifts and the Meaning of Design
    </a>
    – Christmas gifts are a great analogy for understanding the difference between traditional market research and design thinking.
   </li>
  </ul>
  <figure aria-describedby="caption-attachment-630" class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_630" style="width: 600px">
   <a href="http://stratechery.com/2013/christmas-gifts-meaning-design/">

   </a>
   <figcaption class="wp-caption-text" id="caption-attachment-630">
    From “Christmas Gifts and the Meaning of Design”
   </figcaption>
  </figure>
  <p>
   Happy New Year. I’m looking forward to a great 2014.
  </p>
