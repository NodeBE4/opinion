   Shameless Samsung

 <div class="entry-content">
  <p>
   Samsung
   <a href="http://www.theverge.com/2013/9/4/4692824/samsung-galaxy-gear-features-specs-release-date-price">
    introduced
   </a>
   the Galaxy Gear yesterday, and, yeah, it’s not great.
  </p>
  <blockquote align="center" class="twitter-tweet">
   <p>
    Even Samsung fans are mocking the company saying they should have waited for Apple.
   </p>
   <p>
    — Sammy the Walrus IV (@SammyWalrusIV)
    <a href="https://twitter.com/SammyWalrusIV/statuses/375322131955200000">
     September 4, 2013
    </a>
   </p>
  </blockquote>
  <p>
  </p>
  <p>
   I
   <a href="https://twitter.com/monkbent/status/374329003152113664">
    jumped on the leaked prototype
   </a>
   , with the same general sentiment: Samsung’s watch will undoubtedly change drastically whenever Apple’s wearable is released. And, to be certain, we will mock them for copying.
  </p>
  <p>
   Perhaps we’ll even use a staged picture:
  </p>
  <blockquote align="center" class="twitter-tweet">

     March 15, 2013
    </a>
   </p>
  </blockquote>
  <p>
  </p>
  <p>
   Or maybe just a checklist:
  </p>
  <blockquote align="center" class="twitter-tweet">
   <p>
    <a href="https://twitter.com/search?q=%23NothingFeelsBetterThan&amp;src=hash">
     #NothingFeelsBetterThan

    being first…

   </p>
   <p>

    <a href="https://twitter.com/nokia/statuses/312541447289200640">
     March 15, 2013
    </a>
   </p>
  </blockquote>
  <p>
  </p>
  <p>
   Samsung, to their credit, hasn’t responded; if I ran their Twitter account, I’d be tempted to post this:
  </p>
  <figure aria-describedby="caption-attachment-497" class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_497" style="width: 600px">
   <a href="http://ben-evans.com/benedictevans/2013/9/3/nokia-then-and-now">
    <img alt="Via Benedict Evans. Click the image for the original article." class="size-medium wp-image-497" height="597" src="https://stratechery.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/09/Screenshot-2013-09-05-at-11.34.15-PM-600x597.png" width="600"/>
   </a>
   <figcaption class="wp-caption-text" id="caption-attachment-497">
    Via Benedict Evans. Click the image for the original article.
   </figcaption>
  </figure>
  <p>
   Every pre-iPhone phone maker is irrelevant, if they even exist, except for Samsung,
   <em>
    who is thriving
   </em>
   . Samsung the copycat was smart enough to realize they needed to change, and quickly, and so they did.
  </p>
  <p>
   Or maybe it wasn’t being smart. Maybe it was simply not caring what anyone else thought about them, their strategy, or their inspiration. Most successful companies, including Apple,
   <a href="http://stratechery.com/2013/the-tragic-beauty-of-google/">
    including Google
   </a>
   , seem remarkably capable of ignoring the naysayers and simply doing what is right for their company. In the case of smartphones, why
   <em>
    wouldn’t
   </em>
   you copy the iPhone? Nokia
   <a href="http://stratechery.com/2013/blackberry-and-nokias-fundamental-failing/">
    refused
   </a>
   and look where that got them!
  </p>
  <p>
   We, especially in the West, have a powerful sense of justice and fairness when it comes to product features and being first. Business, though, is not fair, even if it is more just than we care to admit.
  </p>
  <p>
   <figure aria-describedby="caption-attachment-496" class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_496" style="width: 600px">
    <a href="http://stratechery.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/09/Samsung_Galaxy_Gear-5468_610x458.jpg">
     <img alt="Notification: You will be discontinued when Apple releases a wearable." class="size-medium wp-image-496" height="450" src="https://stratechery.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/09/Samsung_Galaxy_Gear-5468_610x458-600x450.jpg" width="600"/>
    </a>
    <figcaption class="wp-caption-text" id="caption-attachment-496">
     Notification: You will be discontinued when Apple releases a wearable.
    </figcaption>
   </figure>
   <br>
    Ridiculous? Yes.
   </br>
  </p>
  <p>
   Probability of failure? High.
  </p>
  <p>
   Discontinued after Apple’s launch? Certainly.
  </p>
  <p>
   Liable to put Samsung out of business? In fact, it’s a symbol of exactly why they succeed.
  </p>
